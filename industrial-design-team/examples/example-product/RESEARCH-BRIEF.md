# Research brief — Compass (desk-mounted task light)

## The user

**Primary user:** "Maya, designer/maker hybrid, 5-6 hr at desk per day"
- **Use context:** desk in shared studio; reads physical books AND screens; mixed daylight + overhead fluorescent; needs focused fill light
- **Frequency:** daily; on/off ~10× per session
- **Adjacent objects:** monitor, sketchbook, MacBook, pencil cup; clamp must coexist with monitor arm
- **Skill / familiarity assumed:** prosumer; will replace bulb / re-position weekly
- **Hand size profile:** P50 female-to-P95 male hand, mostly precision-grip operations (small dial, head-rotate)

## The problem

Existing affordable desk lights are either too "office" (corporate task lights — visible, awkward) or too "designer" (Anglepoise — hot, fragile, glare). Maya needs focused, glare-controlled, easy-to-reposition fill that aesthetically belongs on a designer's desk without being precious.

## Constraints

- **Budget (BOM target):** $35 retail-cost → ~$8 BOM at unit volume
- **Production volume target (year 1):** 5,000 units (Kickstarter + direct sales)
- **Manufacturing geography:** Shenzhen-region preferred (small-batch tooling cost)
- **Compliance targets:** UL listed (US), CE (EU), low-voltage USB-C powered (no high-voltage compliance burden)
- **Sustainability targets:** ≥ 30 % recycled-content plastic on body parts; mono-material body (no metal-plastic-rubber co-mould); user-replaceable bulb (LED module standard fitting)
- **Brand constraints:** open — first-party brand of the studio; design-language references include Anglepoise (heritage), Hay (craft), and Muji (restraint)

## Success criteria

- **Functional:**
  - Clamps to desks 12-40 mm thick (covers 95% of common desks)
  - Head reaches 350 mm from clamp pivot in any direction
  - Glare-controlled — direct view of LED at 30° from beam axis < 50 cd/m²
  - Operates on standard USB-C PD 12 V trigger (no proprietary power brick)
  - LED module is industry-standard GU10 or MR16 socket (user-replaceable)
- **Emotional:**
  - "Looks like it belongs on a designer's desk"
  - Clamp action feels reassuring — "thunk" not "wiggle"
  - Head pivot is firm without being stiff
- **Failure mode:**
  - User can't position the head one-handed
  - Light glares into eyes from common desk-sitting postures
  - Clamp slips on textured-edge desks
  - Plastic looks "office supply"

## Out of scope for v1

- Battery operation (USB-C wired only)
- Smart features (no Wi-Fi, app, voice)
- Colour-changing (single warm-white, ~3000 K)
- Floor-stand option
- Wall-mount option

## Adjacent products considered for reference

- Anglepoise Type 75 — heritage, but $$$$ + spring arm noisy
- BenQ ScreenBar — clip-on monitor light, but affixed to monitor, not focused
- IKEA Forså — task light, $25, decent, but plastic-cheap aesthetic
- Wästberg Studioilse — design-forward, $$$, single-piece pure aesthetic
- Custom Anglepoise on Etsy — gives the design-friendly target zone
