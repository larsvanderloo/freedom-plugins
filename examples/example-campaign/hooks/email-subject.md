# Hooks — email subject lines for Infinite Canvas launch

**Brief:** `../brief.md`
**Target persona:** Maya (primary, active users)
**CTA this hook leads to:** "Zoom out in Parallax"
**Emotional state we're reaching for:** mild surprise → curiosity to open and try it
**Char constraint:** 40-50 for mobile-safe preview
**Voice reminder:** no emoji, no "revolutionary", no corporate-we

---

## Set A — Direct / promise-led
1. `Canvas is infinite now.` (24 char) — *states the shipped change with zero wind-up*
2. `The canvas just lost its ceiling.` (33 char) — *same promise, slight metaphor*
3. `Canvas: no more edge.` (21 char) — *staccato, very Maya-voice*

## Set B — Curiosity / pattern-interrupt
4. `You're going to like what zoom-out does now.` (45 char) — *forecasts value without revealing*
5. `We moved an edge.` (17 char) — *minimalist, invites the "which edge?"*
6. `That soft edge on your canvas? Gone.` (37 char) — *calls out the known limitation first*

## Set C — Specific-customer / story-led
7. `The canvas you started in January is still there.` (50 char) — *specific temporal anchor, persona-true*
8. `Merge those split canvases.` (28 char) — *addresses the workaround they currently use*

## Set D — Contrarian / anti-status-quo
9. `Every canvas app boasts about organisation. We removed the need for it.` (73 — TOO LONG)
   → `Your canvases don't need organising now.` (40 char)
10. `You don't need more canvases. You need a bigger one.` (53 — TOO LONG)
    → `You don't need more canvases.` (29 char) — *implied continuation forces open*

## Set E — Number / specificity
11. `10,000 elements. One canvas.` (28 char) — *concrete, matches brand voice's specificity rule*
12. `2019 iPads and up, 10K+ elements, smooth.` (42 char) — *proof-heavy, matches "grounded" trait*

## Set F — Question-led
(skipping — our audience's pain isn't verbatim "is my canvas too big?" — question archetype adds nothing here)

## My picks (3)

### Top pick: #1 — "Canvas is infinite now."
**Why:**
- Matches the email body's opening line exactly — reinforces and rewards the open
- 24 chars — well inside mobile preview
- Voice-correct: specific, grounded, unapologetic, no hyperbole
- Readable in half a second on a locked phone
**Concerns:** None meaningful.

### Runner-up: #4 — "You're going to like what zoom-out does now."
**Why:**
- Curiosity hook without bait-and-switch (we do deliver)
- 45 chars, mobile-safe
- Conversational tone matches past successful email ("Sticker sync across devices")
- Doesn't reveal the feature — works for users who aren't already primed
**Concerns:** Slightly softer voice than #1; borderline "friendly" in a way the brand voice is usually not. Still passes.

### Dark horse: #7 — "The canvas you started in January is still there."
**Why:**
- Specific temporal hook — speaks directly to Maya's emotional JTBD ("avoid the shame of can't find anything")
- 50 chars exactly (mobile-max)
- Narrative-style, likely high-open for emotionally-resonant persona fit
**Concerns:** January reference is seasonal — won't age past ~June. Would need swap for long-lived automated sequences. For a one-shot launch email, fine.
