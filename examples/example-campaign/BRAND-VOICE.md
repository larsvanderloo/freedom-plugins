# Brand Voice — Parallax Notes

## Personality (the 4)

- **Grounded** — we make tangible promises backed by what the product actually does; no hand-waving.
- **Craft-aware** — we write for people who notice kerning and label drift; our copy reflects that care.
- **Dry, not wry** — understated humour, not punchlines. We don't chase laughs.
- **Teammate, not vendor** — we sound like a colleague reviewing a draft over coffee, not a brand "engaging".

## Voice traits — "We sound ..."

- **Specific.** We cite. "3× retention" not "better engagement".
- **Unapologetic about a point of view.** We choose sides on taste questions; we don't hedge.
- **Conversational-spare.** Few commas. Clean rhythm. No throat-clearing.
- **Sensory when relevant.** We describe what the canvas feels like, not just what features exist.

## We NEVER sound ...

- **Hyperbolic.** Banned: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-generation", "the future of X".
- **Productivity-culture earnest.** Banned framings: "unlock your potential", "your second brain", "supercharge".
- **Earnest about "AI".** We avoid leading with "AI-powered". If an AI feature is relevant, we describe the outcome first, then mention AI as a mechanism.
- **Three-adjective-stack.** "Powerful, intuitive, seamless" is banned.
- **Corporate-we.** "We're thrilled to announce..." is banned. Say what we made, not how we feel about it.

## Lexicon

### Always
- "canvas" (the central metaphor)
- "throw it down" (for quick capture)
- "sticky", "card", "shape" (note primitives)
- "thinking" (the activity we support — not "productivity", not "knowledge work")

### Never
- "knowledge base", "PKM", "second brain"
- "productivity suite", "all-in-one"
- "unlock", "supercharge", "level up"
- "empower", "enable" (weak verbs)
- "revolutionary", "game-changing"
- Emoji in headlines (ok in casual social posts sparingly)

## Sample passage (calibration reference)

> Parallax starts every note as a blank canvas. Not a cursor blinking on a line — an empty space you can drop a screenshot into, circle the part that matters, and write around it.
>
> That choice is the whole product. Text apps force you to serialise your thinking. We don't. When you sketch a relationship between three ideas, it stays a spatial sketch. When you need to search it two months later, it's still the sketch you remember — not the bullet list version of what you were trying to say.
>
> Your canvases sync. Your sticker collection travels. Your messy brainstorm from Tuesday lives next to your polished weekly review. Same canvas, different uses.

## Voice calibration examples

| Topic | Wrong voice | Our voice |
|---|---|---|
| New feature — infinite canvas | "We're thrilled to unveil our revolutionary infinite canvas — a game-changing way to supercharge your thinking!" | "Canvas is infinite now. Zoom out far enough and last month's notes are still there, at the edge of this month's." |
| Pricing tier launch | "Unlock premium features with Parallax Pro!" | "Pro lives behind a paywall because one person can't sync five iPads for free and we'd rather charge than gate badly." |
| Sync reliability fix | "We've significantly improved sync performance for an even more seamless experience!" | "Sync was laggy between iPad and Mac on the 1.2.0 release. It isn't now. Details: <link>." |
| Outage post-mortem | "We experienced a brief service interruption. We sincerely apologise for any inconvenience." | "We were down for 47 minutes on Tuesday. Cause: a migration that skipped a step. Detail and what we did: <link>." |
